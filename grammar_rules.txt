1. Ensure proper subject-verb-object (SVO) structure.
   Example: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." is correct. Avoid: "මම කියවමි පොතක්."

2. Avoid redundant particles such as "ක්", "ම", and "ක්වෙ." Use minimal particles.
   Example: "පොතක් කියවමි." is preferred over "පොතක් කියවමිනි."

3. Use correct verb conjugation depending on tense.
   Example: Present: "කැවිමි" Past: "කෑවෙමි" Future: "කනවා."

4. Keep sentence length concise. Break long sentences into simpler parts.
   Example: "මම පොත් දෙකක් කියවා පාඩම් ලිවිය." is preferred over "මම පොත් දෙකක් කියවා සිටිමි එසේ මට පාඩම් ලිවිය."

5. Proper spacing between words is critical.
   Example: Avoid: "මමපොතක්." Correct: "මම පොතක්."

6. Use the correct plural forms for nouns and adjectives.
Example: Singular: "පොතක්" → Plural: "පොත්."
Avoid incorrect forms like: "පොතලා."

7. Ensure agreement between subject and verb in gender and number.
Example: Masculine: "ඔහු කියවයි." Feminine: "ඇය කියවයි."
Incorrect: "ඔහු කියවයි ඇය."

8. Use appropriate honorifics based on context and social norms.
Example: Informal: "ඔබ කුමක් කරයිද?" → Formal: "ඔබ කුමක් කරන්නේද?"
Avoid mixing informal and formal styles in the same sentence.

9. Avoid mixing spoken and written forms in the same sentence.
Example: Spoken: "මට බඩගිනියි." Written: "මට ආහාර අභිලාෂය පවතී."
Do not combine: "මට ආහාර බඩගිනියි."

10. Use the correct cases for nouns (nominative, accusative, etc.) based on context.
Example: Subject: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." Object: "මට පොතක්."
Avoid: "මට කියවමි පොතක්."

11. Avoid overuse of emphasis particles like "ම," "ද," and "එ."
Example: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි" is preferred over "මමම ගමටම ගියෙමිනි."

12. Use proper punctuation to clarify meaning.
Example: Add commas or full stops where appropriate:
Correct: "මම, ගමට ගියෙමි."
Avoid: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි ම."

13. Write numbers in full for formal texts, and use numerals for informal texts.
Example: Formal: "දහය." Informal: "10."
Avoid mixing: "දහය 10."

14. Use correct syntax for interrogative sentences.
Example: Question: "ඔයා කුමක් කරන්නේද?" Avoid: "ඔයා කරන්නේ කුමක්?"

15. Avoid literal translations from other languages.
Example: Correct Sinhala structure: "ඔයා කාලෙකුට පසුව එනවාද?"
Avoid: "ඔයා කල් පසුව එනවාද?" (literal translation of "Are you coming after some time?").

16. Avoid unnecessary repetition of words or ideas.
Example: "මම ගමට ගියෙමි." is preferred over "මම ගමට ගියෙමි ගමට."

17. Correct use of pronouns based on subject, object, or possessive case.
Example: Subject: "ඔහු." Object: "ඔහුව." Possessive: "ඔහුගේ."
Avoid: "ඔහු ඔහුව ඔහුගේ."

18. Use correct conjunctions to link phrases and clauses.
Example: "මම කියවමි සහ ලියමි."
Avoid: "මම කියවමි ලියමි."

19. Ensure appropriate word order for emphasis when needed.
Example: Regular: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." Emphatic: "මම කියවමි පොතක්ම."
Avoid unnecessary emphatic order: "කියවමි මම පොතක්ම."

20. Avoid using colloquial terms in formal writing unless specified.
Example: Formal: "ආහාර ගනු ලැබේ." Colloquial: "කෑම ගන්නවා."
Use consistent tone throughout.

### 1. **Subject-Verb Agreement (කර්තෘ-ක්‍රියාව සම්මුතිය)**
In Sinhala, the verb must agree with the subject in terms of gender, number, and person.

- **Example Rule:**
  - If the subject (කර්තෘ) is singular and masculine, the verb must also be singular and masculine.
  - **Correct Example**: 
    - "මම ගියෙමි" (I went) – Singular, first-person.
  - **Incorrect Example**:
    - "මම ගියා" (informal).

- **General Rule Syntax:**
  - Subject: `සමගාමී`
  - Verb: `[ගියෙමි, ගියෙමු]` (changes according to subject properties)

---

### 2. **Word Order (වචන පිළිවෙළ)**  
Sinhala typically follows the **Subject-Object-Verb (SOV)** word order.

- **Rule Syntax**:
  - `<Subject> <Object> <Verb>`
  - Example:
    - **Correct**: "මම පොතක් කියවමි." (I read a book.)
    - **Incorrect**: "මම කියවමි පොතක්."

---

### 3. **Postpositions and Case Markers (අනුවාදක සහ විභක්තිเครื่อง)**
Postpositions in Sinhala determine the case of the noun.

- **Example Rule**:
  - If a noun represents an object, it must end with the accusative case marker `-ට` or `-ව`.  
    - **Correct**: "මම ගුරුතුමාට ලිපියක් ලිවීය." (I wrote a letter to the teacher.)
    - **Incorrect**: "මම ගුරුතුමා ලිපියක් ලිවීය."

---

### 4. **Use of Honorifics (ගෞරවාන්විත භාෂාව)**  
Honorific verbs and pronouns must be used when referring to respected individuals.

- **Rule Syntax**:
  - If the subject is someone respected, replace neutral verbs with honorific forms.
  - Example:
    - **Correct**: "ගුරුතුමා ගියහ." (The teacher went.)
    - **Incorrect**: "ගුරුතුමා ගියා."

---

### 5. **Negation Rules (නැහැකම් නියමයන්)**  
In Sinhala, negation is formed by adding `නැහැ` or `නෑ` to the end of the verb.

- **Example Rule**:
  - A verb in negative form should not coexist with positive adverbs.
  - **Correct**: "මම කන්නෙ නැහැ." (I do not eat.)
  - **Incorrect**: "මම කන්නෙ නැහැ අතිශය වේගයෙන්." (Logical error: I do not eat very quickly.)

---

### 6. **Pluralization Rules (බහුවචන නියම)**  
Sinhala plural nouns usually take the suffix `-වරු` (for people) or `-ගොඩ` (for objects/animals).

- **Example Rule**:
  - If a noun has the suffix `-වරු`, the verb must agree in plural.
    - **Correct**: "ගුරුවරයාගෙන් පිටවූවරු ගුරුගෙදරට ආවහ." (Teachers came to the staff room.)
    - **Incorrect**: "ගුරුගොඩ."

---

### 7. **Adjective-Noun Agreement (විශේෂණ-නාම සම්මුතිය)**  
In Sinhala, adjectives must agree with nouns in gender and number.

- **Example Rule**:
  - If the noun is singular and feminine, the adjective must be in feminine singular form.
    - **Correct**: "ලස්සන ගැහැණියක්." (A beautiful woman.)
    - **Incorrect**: "ලස්සන ගැහැණිය."

---

### 8. **Tense Agreement (කාල සම්මුතිය)**  
The tense of the verb must match the context of the sentence.

- **Example Rule**:
  - If the context refers to a past event, the verb must be in the past tense.
    - **Correct**: "මම පෙරේදා විදුහලට ගියෙමි." (I went to school yesterday.)
    - **Incorrect**: "මම පෙරේදා විදුහලට යන්නෙමි." (I will go to school yesterday.)


### 1. **Subject-Verb Agreement with "අපි"**
In Sinhala, when "අපි" (we) is the subject, the verb must agree in **plural first-person form**.

- **Example Rule**:
  - If the subject is "අපි," the verb must reflect plural action.
  - **Correct**: "අපි යමු." (We will go.)
  - **Incorrect**: "අපි යනවා." (This is singular or informal usage.)

---

### 2. **Inclusive vs. Exclusive "We" (අපි-අපේ සම්බන්ධතාවය)**
Sinhala distinguishes between **inclusive "අපි"** (we, including the listener) and **exclusive "අපි"** (we, excluding the listener).

- **Example Rule**:
  - Use inclusive language (e.g., "අපි එකට") when including the listener.
    - **Correct**: "අපි එකට යමු." (Let's go together.)
  - Use exclusive language when the listener is not part of the group.
    - **Correct**: "අපි ගෙදරට යමු." (We [not including you] are going home.)

---

### 3. **Use of "අපේ" as Possessive Pronoun**
When "අපි" (we) is used in possessive contexts, it changes to "අපේ" (our).

- **Example Rule**:
  - Possessive nouns must follow "අපේ."
    - **Correct**: "අපේ ගුරුවරයා." (Our teacher.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි ගුරුවරයා."

---

### 4. **Plurality with Objects (අපි සහ වස්තු ප්‍රභේදය)**
When "අපි" (we) is used with objects, the plural form of the object must match the subject's plurality.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Use plural objects for plural subjects.
    - **Correct**: "අපි පොත් කියවමු." (We read books.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි පොත කියවමු." (We read a book.) – Mismatch in plurality.

---

### 5. **Politeness and Formality with "අපි"**
In formal contexts, the verbs associated with "අපි" should reflect politeness or respect.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Add polite suffixes like "මු" or "මුහුන්."
    - **Correct**: "අපි යමු." (Let us go.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි යනවා." (Informal or conversational tone.)

---

### 6. **Negation with "අපි"**
For negations, "අපි" takes verbs in their negative form, often ending in "නෑ" or "නැහැ."

- **Example Rule**:
  - Negation must follow "අපි" directly.
    - **Correct**: "අපි කන්නෙ නැහැ." (We do not eat.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි කෑම නැහැ." (Incorrect form for negation.)

---

### 7. **Verb Tense with "අපි"**
The verb tense for "අපි" must align with the time frame of the action.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Use future tense for future actions.
    - **Correct**: "අපි වැඩට යන්නෙමු." (We will go to work.)
  - Use past tense for past actions.
    - **Correct**: "අපි වැඩට ගියෙමු." (We went to work.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි වැඩට යන්නෙමු." (Logical error in tense.)

---

### 8. **Imperative Sentences with "අපි"**
When giving commands or making requests involving "අපි," verbs should be in imperative form with inclusivity.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Use the inclusive imperative.
    - **Correct**: "අපි එකට කෑම කමු." (Let us eat together.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි කෑම කනවා." (Not imperative.)

---

### 9. **Emphasis and Reflexivity with "අපිම"**
The emphatic form "අපිම" (we ourselves) is used to emphasize the subject.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Use "අපිම" when emphasizing the action by "we."
    - **Correct**: "අපිම වැඩේ කළෙමු." (We ourselves did the work.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි වැඩේ කළෙමු." (Lacks emphasis.)

---

### 10. **Coordination with Other Pronouns (අපි සහ අනෙකුත් පදයෝගය)**
When combining "අපි" with other pronouns or nouns, conjunctions like "සහ" or "එකතුවෙන්" must be used.

- **Example Rule**:
  - Combine using appropriate conjunctions.
    - **Correct**: "අපි සහ ඔවුන් ගියෙමු." (We and they went.)
    - **Incorrect**: "අපි ඔවුන් ගියෙමු." (Missing conjunction.)
